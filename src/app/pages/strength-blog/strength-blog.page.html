What are the Benefits of Strength Training?

Strength training increases bone density, builds a stronger heart, reduces your resting blood pressure, improves blood flow, halts muscle loss, helps control blood sugar, improves cholesterol levels, and improves your balance and coordination.

When you finish a workout, your body needs to do a lot of work to replenish itself in order to bring itself back to a normal state (the way it was before you worked out). This takes a lot of energy, and some studies have shown that it can boost your metabolism for up to 38 hours after you finish your workout./p>

Who SHOULDN’T strength train?

Trying to be balanced, I wanted to find studies of a single group of people who should not strength train. But then I found studies on how strength training can be beneficial for paraplegics.I also found studies that show the benefits for children and adolescents.

I next found plenty of studies that explain the benefits of strength training for pregnant women.

And thus I’ve decided, not knowing you, you should probably strength train. Of course, check with your doctor before you start any sort of strength training program (though, as you’ll see below, strength training doesn’t need to be scary or complicated).
